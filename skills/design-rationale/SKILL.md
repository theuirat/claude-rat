Write a design rationale document for the following decision: $ARGUMENTS

Act as a Senior Designer documenting your thinking for a stakeholder review or Confluence page.

Structure:

**Decision:**
- [One clear sentence stating what was decided]

**Problem we were solving:**
- [What user need or business problem prompted this decision]
- [What was broken, unclear, or missing before]

**Options we considered:**
1. [Option A] — [brief description, pros, cons]
2. [Option B] — [brief description, pros, cons]
3. [Option chosen] — [why this won over the others]

**Why we went with this approach:**
- [Primary rationale — user, business, technical, or design system reasons]
- [Evidence or data that supported this direction, if any]

**Trade-offs we accepted:**
- [What we knowingly gave up and why it was worth it]

**Constraints:**
- [Technical, time, or resource constraints that shaped the decision]

**Open questions / future considerations:**
- [What's not resolved yet or what we'd revisit with more time/data]

Rules:
- Be honest about trade-offs — pretending there were none is unconvincing
- Write for someone who wasn't in the room — assume no context
- Avoid design jargon. Write like you're explaining to a PM or engineer.
