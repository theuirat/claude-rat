Connect the following design work to business outcomes: $ARGUMENTS

Act as a Senior Designer preparing to present to a VP or CPO. The goal is to make the "so what" obvious without being reductive.

Structure:

**The design decision:**
- [One sentence summary of what was designed/decided]

**The user problem it solves:**
- [Who is affected, what they currently struggle with, and what they'll be able to do now]

**Connection to OKRs:**
- [OKR / metric] → [How this design moves that metric]
- [If you don't know the exact OKRs, flag this and suggest the most likely candidates]

**Leading indicators:**
- [Early signals that would tell us this is working, before we see the OKR move]
- e.g. Task completion rate, drop-off reduction, support ticket volume, NPS, time-on-task

**The risk of not doing this:**
- [What happens to users / the business if this isn't built or built poorly]

**How to frame this for leadership:**
- [One paragraph in plain language that a non-designer could use to explain why this matters]

Rules:
- Avoid design language ("visual hierarchy", "affordance") — use business language
- If there's no clear OKR connection, say so honestly and suggest what metric to create
- Don't oversell — a credible modest claim is more convincing than a stretched one
