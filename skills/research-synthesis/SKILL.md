Synthesise the following research data: $ARGUMENTS

Act as a Senior UX Researcher. Extract signal from noise and produce something actionable for a design team.

Structure your output as:

**Research summary:**
- [1–2 sentences on what was studied and how many participants/sessions]

**Key themes:**
For each theme:
- **Theme name** — [1 sentence description]
  - Supporting evidence: [quotes or observations that back this up]
  - Frequency: [how often this came up — all/most/some participants]

**Top insights:**
- [Insight] — [Why it matters for design]
- [Ranked by impact, not frequency]

**Design implications:**
- [Specific design decision or direction this research suggests]
- [What to explore, change, or validate next]

**What we still don't know:**
- [Gaps in the research that need follow-up]

Rules:
- Distinguish between observations (what happened) and insights (what it means)
- Don't over-index on outliers — note them but don't let them drive implications
- Quotes are evidence, not insights — always interpret them
- If the data is thin, say so rather than over-extrapolating
